📊 AI Benchmark Hub

Leaderboards and evaluation platforms for comparing frontier AI models

Why so many? These benchmarks don't really disagree — they measure different things. Some focus on reasoning, others on coding, math, conversational quality, safety, or real-world usefulness. A model might top one leaderboard and lag on another. For a rounded view of any frontier model, cross-reference two or three benchmarks that match your use case. Note: some entries below link to a benchmark's description page rather than a live leaderboard — that's because many widely-cited benchmarks (MMLU, GPQA, MATH) were published as datasets, not hosted dashboards. In those cases we link to Papers with Code for live rankings.
Head-to-Head Arenas — Human Preference
LMArena
Blind pairwise voting produces Elo ratings across chat, vision, and coding. The de facto "real-world quality" leaderboard
Free
WebDev Arena
Head-to-head evaluation of models on full-stack web development prompts, judged by users
Free
Dynabench
Dynamic, adversarial evaluation where humans generate challenge examples that fool current models
Free
Holistic & Multi-Dimensional Evaluations

Evaluates accuracy, robustness, fairness, efficiency, and more across many scenarios
Free
Artificial Analysis
Frontier model comparison across intelligence, speed, price, and context — great single-page overview
Free
LiveBench
Contamination-free benchmark with questions refreshed monthly across reasoning, math, coding, and more
Free
Papers with Code
Aggregates leaderboards across hundreds of NLP, vision, and ML benchmarks, linked to source papers
Free
Reasoning & Knowledge

Free

Expert-authored questions across 100+ subjects designed to challenge the strongest frontier models
Free

57-subject multitask test. MMLU-Pro is the harder, less-saturated successor with live rankings
Free

Google's 200+ task benchmark spanning reasoning, creativity, and linguistics. BBH is the hard subset
